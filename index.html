We don't always have to have all the sides of our padding, borders, and margins matching. There are a number of ways to be a bit more unique! We can use properties to pinpoint sides - we can add "-top", "-bottom", "-left", and "-right" to our padding, borders, or margins.
We can also use shorthand to apply many values to one property! The values work in a clockwise direction, starting from the top, so:
padding: 20px; would have all sides as 20px
padding: 20px 50px; would have the top and bottom paddings as 20px, but left and right sides as 50px
padding: 20px 50px 40px 10px; would work clockwise. Top is 20px, right is 50px, bottom is 40px, and finally left is 10px.
The margins are 0px on the top, 15px on the bottom, 20px to the left, and 60px to the right.

Level 6 - Flexbox

Welcome to Level 6, where we take a look at Flexbox! If you haven't covered Flexbox in class yet, don't worry! You can come back to this level later. Flexbox is CSS syntax we can use to lay our content out in an easier way than before. We can tell it to put our content into rows or columns, and then from that we can can tell flexbox how to arrange our items - do we want space around the items? Do we want them at the top of the container? It's very flexible!
